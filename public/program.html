Program Logic
Why 9 days
Decouples training from the calendar. No more "leg day is always Saturday." Each cycle repeats on its own schedule.
Push and Pull twice
Chest hits Push A + Push B. Biceps hit Pull A + Pull B. 2x frequency per muscle group is the sweet spot for hypertrophy.
Cardio placement
Zone 2 on Day 2 aids recovery after Push A. VO2 on Day 4 sits between Pull A and Rest — hard effort, then a full recovery day after.
Cardio after lifting
20 min Zone 2 treadmill ends each lifting session. Low enough intensity it won't blunt hypertrophy signaling.

VO2
VO2 Max Day — Norwegian 4×4 Intervals
Day 4 · ~35–40 min total
⌄
What is VO2 max training? Your VO2 max is the maximum rate your body can consume oxygen during exercise. Training it means repeatedly pushing your cardiovascular system to near its ceiling (90–95% max HR) and letting it recover, then doing it again. The Norwegian 4×4 protocol is the most studied and effective method — 4 minutes hard, 3 minutes easy, repeated 4 times. Once per cycle produces measurable gains within 6–8 weeks.
Warmup
10 MIN
Easy jog. HR building to ~60–70% max. Loosen up.
Main Block
× 4 ROUNDS
Hard interval
4 MIN
Run at 90–95% max HR. Should feel very hard. Talking in single words only.
Active recovery
3 MIN
Slow jog or brisk walk. HR drops to ~60–70%.
Total work block: 28 minutes
Cooldown
5 MIN
Walk or very easy jog. HR returning to normal.
Heart Rate Zones — Estimate your max HR as 220 minus your age
Zone 150–60% max HRVery easy, recovery
Zone 260–70% max HREasy, conversational — your daily cardio
Zone 370–80% max HRModerate, harder to talk
Zone 480–90% max HRHard, threshold work
Zone 5 — VO2 target90–95% max HRNear max. This is where VO2 adaptation happens.
Practical tip: If you don't have a HR monitor, use perceived effort. Zone 5 should feel like a 9/10 — you can run but not sprint, and you cannot hold a conversation. By the 3rd or 4th interval, the 4-minute hard effort should feel genuinely brutal. If it doesn't, go faster.

Progression
Lifting progression
Add 2.5–5 lbs when you hit the top of the rep range for all sets. Don't add weight until you've earned it.
VO2 max progression
After 4–6 weeks, increase the run speed during intervals rather than adding rounds. 4×4 is the proven structure — don't mess with it.
Deload
Every 4–6 cycles, take one cycle at 50–60% volume. This is when adaptation actually consolidates. Don't skip it.
Calendar flexibility
The 9-day cycle doesn't align to weeks — that's intentional. Track by day number, not day of the week.
